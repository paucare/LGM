What is Radiohead?

Radiohead are an English rock band formed in Abingdon, Oxfordshire, in 1985. They comprise Thom Yorke (vocals, guitar, piano, keyboards); brothers Jonny Greenwood (guitar, keyboards, other instruments) and Colin Greenwood (bass); Ed O'Brien (guitar, backing vocals); and Philip Selway (drums, percussion). They have worked with the producer Nigel Godrich and the cover artist Stanley Donwood since 1994. Radiohead's experimental approach is credited with advancing the sound of alternative rock.

Our history

Formation: Radiohead formed in 1985 in Abingdon, Oxfordshire, England. The band originally consisted of Thom Yorke (vocals, guitar, piano), Jonny Greenwood (guitar, keyboards, other instruments), Colin Greenwood (bass), Ed O'Brien (guitar, backing vocals), and Phil Selway (drums, percussion).

Early Years: In the late 1980s and early 1990s, Radiohead gained attention with their debut single "Creep" in 1992, which became a worldwide hit. Their debut album "Pablo Honey" (1993) received mixed reviews but established them as a promising band.

Breakthrough: Radiohead's second album, "The Bends" (1995), showcased a more expansive and experimental sound, receiving critical acclaim and establishing them as a leading force in alternative rock. Singles like "Fake Plastic Trees" and "High and Dry" gained significant radio play.

Exploration and Experimentation: With their third album, "OK Computer" (1997), Radiohead achieved widespread acclaim and commercial success. The album's complex themes, innovative production, and blend of rock and electronic elements marked a significant evolution in their sound. "OK Computer" is often regarded as one of the greatest albums of all time.

Continued Evolution: Radiohead continued to push musical boundaries with subsequent albums like "Kid A" (2000) and "Amnesiac" (2001), which embraced electronic, experimental, and avant-garde influences. These albums were met with critical acclaim and further solidified Radiohead's reputation for innovation.

Later Years: In the 2000s and beyond, Radiohead continued to release critically acclaimed albums such as "Hail to the Thief" (2003), "In Rainbows" (2007), "The King of Limbs" (2011), and "A Moon Shaped Pool" (2016). They also became known for their unconventional release strategies, such as offering "In Rainbows" as a pay-what-you-want download.

Legacy: Radiohead's influence extends beyond music, as they've been praised for their activism, environmental awareness, and commitment to artistic integrity. They've inspired countless bands and musicians across various genres.

Overall, Radiohead's history is characterized by artistic innovation, musical experimentation, and a dedication to pushing boundaries in both music and culture.
